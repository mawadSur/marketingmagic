## Targeting notes

- **All 10 handles are verified real and currently active**, each tied to a documented current project (not a dormant account). Where a handle was uncertain I confirmed it against the person's own site/podcast/WIP/Product Hunt rather than guessing.
- **One correction worth flagging:** my first guess for Marie Martens was `@anthilemoon` — that is *wrong* (that handle belongs to Anne-Laure Le Cunff). Verified handle is **@MarieMartens**. This is the kind of error that makes a DM look mail-merged, so it's caught here.
- **Reality check on yield:** this list skews toward established names (Levels, Marc Lou, Arvid, Damon, Jon, Tony already run $1M+ businesses). They're the most *recognizable* fit but the *least likely to say yes* to a cold design-partner DM — they have their own audiences and entrenched tooling. Treat #1, #3, #5, #6, #10 as the realistic responders and the marquee names as low-probability/high-signal long shots.
- **Sub-segment most likely to bite:** **volume / multi-product builders** (Marc Köhlbrugge's "100 startups," John Rush's 25-product empire, Tony Dinh's portfolio). For them "I physically cannot write content for every launch" is an acute, present pain — the value prop lands without explanation. The single-flagship founders feel it less because one product = manageable by hand.
- **Recommended next step for a *higher-yield* list:** the strongest design partners are usually **mid-tier (1k–20k followers) builders actively posting #buildinpublic this week** — they have the pain, lack the in-house solution, and are flattered by the ask. I could not reliably verify specific small accounts' current activity from web search alone (X profile pages don't render for fetch, and Nitter is dead). If Mohammed pulls 15–20 current #buildinpublic posters from his own X feed, I can write the same personalized DMs against verified, in-the-trenches accounts — likely a much better hit rate than this name-brand list.
